About Me Page

My name is Kyler Martin-Yeboah, and I am a 22-year-old Junior Software Systems Engineer at Technology Service Corporation. While at Louisiana State University (LSU) (2021-2025), I majored in Computer Science with a concentration in Software Engineering and a minor in Digital Media & Arts. I am orginally from Baltimore, Maryland where I lived until leaving for college at LSU in Baton Rouge, Louisiana. I was exposed to the computer science field at a young age after attending various cybersecurity and coding camps which inspired me to pursue this career path.

I chose to major in Computer Science because I liked the creative and collaborative atmosphere aimed at increasing future technological potential. Computer Science is a very diverse field that contains many different aspects all needing separate coding languages and algorithms to perform the prospective function. The freedom to choose which areas intrigue me, learn new ways to advance technology while still being able to incorporate my own design or structure ideas, and stay up to date on the current available technology which may help streamline or generate new projects; are some other reasons I admire the career path. I'd like to think my affinity for sci-fi also plays apart in my pursuit of the computer science field as many of the fictional aspects of sci-fi movies and shows relate to computer science in various ways. Overall, I have a large vested interest in learning as much as I can about computer science and contributing my ideas to grow and advance the field for the future.
